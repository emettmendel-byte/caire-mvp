### **1. Key Clinical Recommendations**
- **Inhaled Corticosteroids (ICS):**
  - **Position in Therapy:** ICS are the cornerstone of long-term asthma control due to their anti-inflammatory effects. They should be used daily at the lowest effective dose to minimize side effects.
  - **Dosing Considerations:** Doses should be titrated based on patient response, with gradual reduction once asthma control is achieved. Growth in children may be slightly delayed but is generally not clinically significant compared to uncontrolled asthma.
  - **Patient Education:** Emphasize daily use, proper inhaler technique (e.g., using a spacer), and rinsing the mouth after use to prevent thrush.

- **Long-Acting Beta-Agonists (LABAs):**
  - **Combination Use:** LABAs (e.g., salmeterol, formoterol) should only be used in combination with ICS, not as monotherapy. They enhance lung function but carry risks of severe exacerbations.
  - **Black Box Warnings:** Due to increased risks of asthma-related deaths and exacerbations, LABAs require FDA warnings. Daily use should not exceed 100 mcg salmeterol or 24 mcg formoterol.
  - **Indications:** LABAs are not recommended for acute symptoms or as standalone controllers.

## Red-team Targeted Fixes
### **Key Points from the Document:**

#### **Inhaled Corticosteroids (ICS)**
1. **Effectiveness**: ICSs are the most effective medications for long-term control of persistent asthma. They target lung inflammation when used daily.
2. **Usage**: Should be taken every day, even without symptoms, to prevent attacks.
3. **Dosing**: Use the lowest effective dose to minimize side effects. Dose should be titrated down once asthma is controlled.
4. **Side Effects**:
   - **Common**: Mouth irritation/thrush (avoided by rinsing after use).
   - **Growth**: May slightly slow linear growth in children, but this is generally small and not progressive.
   - **Other**: Benefits outweigh risks when used correctly.
5. **Safety**: Generally safe for pregnant women and non-addictive. Not the same as anabolic steroids.

---

#### **Long-Acting Beta-2 Agonists (LABAs)**
1. **Combination with ICS**: LABAs (e.g., salmeterol, formoterol) are added to asthma treatment when low-dose ICS alone is insufficient.
2. **Benefits**: Improve lung function, reduce symptoms, and decrease asthma exacerbations.
3. **Risks**:
   - **Black Box Warning**: FDA placed warnings due to increased risk of severe exacerbations (e.g., salmeterol trial showed higher mortality).
   - **Usage**: Should not be used alone for long-term control. Must be combined with ICS.
   - **Dosage Limit**: Daily use should not exceed 100 mcg salmeterol or 24 mcg formoterol.
4. **Acute Symptoms**: LABAs are not recommended for treating sudden asthma attacks.

---

#### **Patient Education**
1. **ICS**:
   - Emphasize daily use for prevention.
   - Teach proper inhaler technique and rinsing to avoid side effects.
   - Address misconceptions (e.g., ICSs do not slow growth significantly compared to uncontrolled asthma).
2. **LABAs**:
   - Explain the importance of combining LABAs with ICS.
   - Highlight the risks of using LABAs alone or for acute symptoms.
