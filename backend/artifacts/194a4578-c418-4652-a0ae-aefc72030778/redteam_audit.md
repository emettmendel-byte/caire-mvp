## I. Dosing Guidelines & Treatment Principles

1.  **Inhaled Corticosteroids (ICS):**
    *   **Standard of Care:** Dose is individualized based on severity and response. Start low and increase as needed.
    *   **Combination Therapy:** Often used in combination with other controllers (e.g., long-acting beta-agonists, leukotriene modifiers, theophylline).
    *   **Growth Concern:** May slightly reduce growth velocity in children, but the benefits of asthma control generally outweigh this risk if asthma is well-controlled.
    *   **Usage:** Daily use is crucial for effective control and reducing inflammation, even when symptoms are absent.

2.  **Long-Acting Beta-Agonists (LABAs):**
    *   **Combination Required:** MUST be used *only* in combination with an ICS. They are not recommended as monotherapy for persistent asthma.
    *   **Dosing Limit:** The maximum recommended daily dose is 100 mcg of salmeterol or 24 mcg of formoterol.
    *   **Risk:** Carry a significant risk of severe asthma exacerbations (Black Box Warning). This risk increases with higher doses.
    *   **Not for Acute Use:** Should not be used to treat sudden asthma symptoms or attacks; they are for long-term control.

## II. Patient Education & Communication

1.  **ICS:**
    *   **Emphasize Daily Use:** Explain that asthma is a chronic condition requiring daily medication for control, not just when symptomatic.
    *   **Proper Technique:** Crucial for effectiveness. May need training.
    *   **Side Effects:** Discuss potential side effects (e.g., hoarseness, oral thrush - mitigated by rinsing mouth) and reassure about common misconceptions (not addictive, not steroids).
    *   **Growth:** Address concerns about growth impact transparently.

2.  **LABAs:**
    *   **Explain Combination:** Clearly state that LABAs *must* be used with an ICS.
    *   **Highlight Risk:** Explain the increased risk of severe asthma attacks associated with LABAs, especially if not combined with an ICS or used at high doses.
    *   **Black Box Warning:** Make patients aware of this significant safety warning.
    *   **Clarify Use:** Stress that LABAs are for *long-term control*, not for *relief* of acute symptoms.
