**Overall Summary:**

The evidence primarily focuses on the management of pediatric diarrhea, particularly acute diarrhea, dehydration, and related complications. Key recommendations and findings include:

*   **Oral Rehydration Solution (ORS):** Essential for managing dehydration from diarrhea. WHO recommends ORS as the primary treatment.
*   **Zinc Supplementation:** Crucial for reducing the duration and severity of diarrhea episodes in children. Zinc is strongly recommended alongside ORS for non-severe diarrhea.
*   **Antibiotics:** Generally not recommended for non-severe diarrhea as it doesn't significantly reduce duration but increases resistance risk. Reserved for specific cases like bloody diarrhea or suspected bacterial infection.
*   **Home Management:** For non-severe dehydration, oral rehydration and zinc can often be managed at home with close monitoring.
*   **Lactose-Free Formulas:** May be considered for short-term use (2-4 weeks) in infants with persistent diarrhea who are not tolerating regular formulas, but evidence is limited.
*   **Lactose-Replete ORS (LORS):** Compared to standard ORS, LORS shows minimal differences in key outcomes like mortality or dehydration resolution, but may slightly reduce stool output.
*   **Enhanced Care:** Hospitalization and close monitoring can be beneficial for certain high-risk children (e.g., very young, malnourished), but the evidence for extended follow-up after discharge is less clear.
