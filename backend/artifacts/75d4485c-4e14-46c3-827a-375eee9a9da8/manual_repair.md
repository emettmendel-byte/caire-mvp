This document is a comprehensive asthma care quick reference guide designed for healthcare professionals, patients, and their families. It provides essential information on managing persistent asthma, focusing on medications for long-term control, particularly inhaled corticosteroids (ICS) and long-acting beta-2 agonists (LABAs). 

Key points include:
- **Inhaled Corticosteroids (ICS)**: The most effective medication for long-term asthma control. They target airway inflammation directly and should be used daily. Common side effects like mouth irritation can be avoided with proper technique, including rinsing the mouth after use. ICS may slightly affect growth in children, but this is generally minimal and not progressive.
- **Long-Acting Beta-2 Agonists (LABAs)**: Should only be used in combination with ICS, not as monotherapy. They carry a Black Box warning due to an increased risk of severe asthma exacerbations, though their benefits often outweigh the risks when used appropriately.
- **Patient Education**: Addresses common misconceptions, such as ICS being addictive or causing significant growth issues, clarifying that these are not major concerns when asthma is well-managed.
- **Dosing and Monitoring**: Emphasizes that once asthma is controlled, the dose should be reduced to the minimum necessary to maintain control, based on the clinician's judgment and patient response.
- **Resources**: Lists numerous educational websites and organizations for further information, including the National Heart, Lung, and Blood Institute (NHLBI), Allergy & Asthma Network, and others.

This guide serves as a practical tool for both healthcare providers and patients, ensuring safe and effective asthma management through clear guidelines, patient education, and access to additional resources.
